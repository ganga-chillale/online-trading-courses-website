Trading Classes - Beginner Level

Basic Terminology

Here are some basic trading terminologies that are essential for beginners:
1. Asset Anything that can be traded, such as stocks, bonds, currencies, or commodities.
2. Stock A share of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a piece of that company.
3. Bond A debt instrument where an investor loans money to an entity (corporate or government) that borrows the funds for a defined period at a fixed interest rate.
4. Commodity A raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold, such as gold, oil, or wheat.
5. Forex (Foreign Exchange) The global marketplace for buying and selling currencies.
6. Cryptocurrency A digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security, like Bitcoin or Ethereum.
7. Broker A person or firm that arranges transactions between a buyer and a seller for a commission when the deal is executed.
8. Exchange A marketplace where financial instruments (stocks, bonds, commodities, etc.) are bought and sold, such as the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) or NASDAQ.
9. Market Order An order to buy or sell a security immediately at the best available current price.
10. Limit Order An order to buy or sell a security at a specific price or better. A buy limit order can only be executed at the limit price or lower, and a sell limit order can only be executed at the limit price or higher.
11. Bid and Ask Bid: The price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.
Ask: The price a seller is willing to accept for a security.
12. Spread The difference between the bid and ask price of a security.
13. Leverage The use of borrowed funds to increase the potential return of an investment. It can amplify both gains and losses.
14. Margin The amount of money that must be deposited with a broker as collateral to cover the risk of loss in trading with leverage.
15. Bull Market A market condition where prices are rising or expected to rise, typically associated with investor confidence and economic growth.
16. Bear Market A market condition where prices are falling or expected to fall, often associated with pessimism and economic slowdown.
17. Volatility A statistical measure of the dispersion of returns for a given security or market index. It represents the degree of variation in trading prices over time.
18. Portfolio A collection of financial investments like stocks, bonds, commodities, and cash equivalents owned by an individual or institution.
19. Diversification The practice of spreading investments across various financial instruments, industries, or other categories to reduce risk.
20. Dividend A portion of a company's earnings distributed to shareholders, usually in the form of cash or additional shares.
